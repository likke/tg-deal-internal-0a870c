● AI Ready Certified · how the credential is earned

A credential that's earned, not handed out

This is what makes "AI Ready Certified" real and defensible next to Singapore's assessed certs (AI4S / AI4I). Every learner is assessed, every certificate is verifiable, and the credential stacks across the pathway. Only TTV-certified trainers may assess.

Assessed
project + knowledge check + presentation — not attendance
Verifiable
unique cert ID + public verify page
Stackable
one credential per pathway level, building over time

One standard, scaled by level. Same three things are always assessed — understanding, responsible use, and a real build — just deeper as the learner climbs. That's what lets the credential stack.
02 /The three assessment instruments
60%
The Build
A real, AI-assisted project/output the learner makes. The core of every level.
20%
Knowledge check
Short quiz/oral on AI concepts + responsible use. Scaled to age.
20%
Presentation
Learner presents and explains their work — the confidence outcome.
Weights flex by level. Elementary leans almost entirely on Build + Present (knowledge check is light/visual). Senior High & College carry the full 60/20/20. Advanced adds a defense to the presentation.

RegistryTTV holds the master registry of issued credentials + certified trainers (the asset that compounds the standard)
Validity / re-certLearner credential = lifetime by level; trainers re-certify yearly (₱10k) to teach the current-year curriculum
This is the moat. A certificate of attendance can be printed by anyone. A verifiable, assessed, registry-backed credential designed by an award-winning founder is the thing schools pay a premium for — and it's owned by TTV.
05 /Integrity & quality control

Certified assessors only

Only TTV-certified trainers may assess and issue credentials — the same gate that protects the IP protects the standard.

Moderation sampling

A sample of each cohort's scored projects is reviewed by TTV against the rubric, to keep scoring consistent across schools and trainers.

Annual refresh

Assessment items + project briefs refresh each year with the curriculum (funded by the ₱300k license) so the credential never goes stale.
